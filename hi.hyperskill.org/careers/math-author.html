Math Projects Author
Educational project Hyperskill is looking for project authors, namely people who can help us teach future programmers (and not only them). Our aim is to make mathematics look understandable and interesting so that people who need it professionally would learn and use it without trepidation and fear, even if they are still far from mathematics. It is not an easy task, you know :)
Primary Responsibilities:

  • Suggest projects ideas for math tracks: https://hyperskill.org/tracks?category=6;
  • Describe a project, divide it into stages, and create project tasks;
  • Update project according to team feedback and user reports.

Some content examples are below:

  • Here is what our theoretical paper (topic) looks like: https://stepik.org/lesson/625877/step/1.
  • And here is an example of a math project: https://stepik.org/lesson/583372/step/1.
  • The published topic looks like this one: https://hyperskill.org/learn/step/16969.
  • And the published project intro page is: https://hyperskill.org/projects/280.
We are waiting for those who:

  • Understand and love mathematics;
  • Can explain complex topics simply and clearly;
  • Speak English at least at the Intermediate level;
  • Have experience in applying math tools to solve practical cases or experience in teaching, mentoring;
  • Would like to try themselves in the field of online education and can allocate time for this.
We offer:

  • Contract agreement with payment for each project created and published;
  • Work at your own pace;
  • Opportunity to study at Hyperskill for free;
  • Help and support from the crew;
  • Experience in an international distributed team.
We are always glad to meet new people!
To apply for the opening, send your CV to join@hyperskill.org with the job title in the subject line.
