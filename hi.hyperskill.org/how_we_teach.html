JetBrains Academy manifesto
We believe that programming is about knowing concrete tools and concepts as well as possessing a set of mental skills that can help you solve complex problems.
We also believe that it's important to develop these skills in a setting that resembles the process of real software development.

The learning principles we're building on
Active learning
Skills are developed by trying out and applying concepts right away to real-life problems. That's why each theoretical piece you read is always followed by practical assignments. Reading or watching educational content without applying it right away has proved to be enjoyable but ineffective.
Knowledge transfer
What we're really after is the knowledge transfer - your ability to use what you've learned on JetBrains Academy in different contexts. This is why we make a big emphasis on learning the tools and the processes of actual development.
Extensive practice
First you solve a few similar problems in each topic so that you fully master the concept and automate the cases discussed in the topic to the point of getting bored. All of that is needed so that when you're faced with applying this concept in a stage of your project you can solely focus on transferring your knowledge to a different context, not remembering the parts and features of the concept.
Project-based learning
Your learning is driven by a concrete challenge posed by the project. This way you get to experience context when a certain tool or concept is applicable.
A project is a big and complex problem, that's why we broke it up in stages and decomposed it for you.
Social learning
We'd love to build a community of active learners that help each other succeed. Working with other people and solving problems together is an invaluable skill for your career as a software developer. Or any career for that matter.
Driven by practical goals
The projects offered by the platform range from simple introductory programs to full swing working apps. We believe that the best kind of motivation stems from your desire to build something real and something that matters to you. That's why at some point you can pick and choose things you can build and focus on something that sparks your interest, be it back-end development, app development of something else.
Building project from simple to complex once
Each stage of a project poses a challenge to you.
Topics in your curriculum help you gather the knowledge to withstand this challenge.
Each stage grows in complexity teaching you how to reuse and expand your previous code while also letting you experience reverse decomposition and get the feeling of how you should break up problems in the future.
